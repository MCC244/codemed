What I've Learned So Far

Python

I studied Python for six years, both in high school and during my preparatory cycle. Throughout this period, I gained a solid foundation in the language. In the second year of my cycle, I also had the opportunity to take a course with a well-respected professor, which further honed my skills and deepened my understanding of Python's versatility in solving problems and creating applications.

HTML

During the first few months of learning HTML, I quickly grasped its concepts and how to effectively structure web pages. Understanding the basic tags, attributes, and how they come together to create a website became intuitive. HTML has become one of the core skills I use regularly to build websites.

CSS

Just like HTML, I found CSS easy to pick up in the first couple of months. It allowed me to bring the designs to life, adding colors, layouts, and responsiveness to the websites I create. With a good understanding of selectors, properties, and box model principles, I can style websites efficiently and with flair.

JavaScript

Although I’m still not an expert in JavaScript, I’m taking it step by step. I’m working through its concepts, and it’s starting to make more sense. JavaScript’s flexibility and the interactivity it brings to web pages have always intrigued me, and I’m excited to keep improving my skills with each new project.

SQL

Last year, I had to learn SQL, and I found it surprisingly easy to understand, especially compared to other languages. However, it requires a lot of concentration and attention to detail when managing databases. I’ve enjoyed learning how to query and manipulate data, and I’m continually improving my proficiency in SQL to better manage complex databases.
